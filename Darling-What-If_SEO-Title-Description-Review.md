# SEO Review — Titles & Meta Descriptions
### 3 drafts (fixes applied) + 12 latest live posts (recommended)

## The two systemic problems I found

**1. Titles are getting truncated in Google.** Search results cut titles at ~60 characters. Every live post carries a `| Darling, What If` suffix, and several titles are long and lyrical on top of that — so the important words get chopped. Worst offenders: *Stress/Anxiety/Depression* (**118 chars**), *Choosing Comfort* (**96**), *Hormone Foods* (**89**).
> **Fix:** drop the `| Darling, What If` suffix on long titles (Google often appends your site name automatically anyway) and front-load the searchable keyword. Keep the suffix only where the title is already short.

**2. Every meta description is too long.** Google shows ~155–160 characters. All 12 live descriptions run **186–264 chars**, so the payoff sentence never shows. Trimming them also lets you front-load keywords.

**3. A few titles are beautiful but unsearchable.** Lyrical titles ("What Japan Taught Me…") read wonderfully but match nothing people type. Where possible I've injected the actual search term (Japanese minimalism, hormone-balancing foods, skin barrier repair) *without* losing your voice.

### Quick rules to keep going forward
- **Title:** ≤ 60 characters, primary keyword in the first few words, a number if it's a list.
- **Description:** ≤ 155 characters, lead with the keyword, end with a reason to click.
- **Drop the brand suffix** unless the title is short — Google adds your site name for you.
- **One trending term where it fits** (green flags, clear-coding, Japanese minimalism) — but never at the cost of clarity.
